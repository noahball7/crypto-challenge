## Operation Clues

- `hole`: Ringo's Sea of Holes object is the actual solution. It bypasses force and functions as a portal/opening in the glass globe.
- `break`: The wording creates ambiguity between physical breakage and a musical drum break.
- `shatter`: Implies glass destruction, but the successful action is not smashing; it is applying the hole.
- `drum break`: Points to drums/Ringo/music, but the absence of drumsticks redirects to pocket inventory.
- `blue glass`: The target container is specifically described as blue glass; blue may be meaningful given Blue Meanies/Pepperland color logic.
- `Beatle-proof`: A resistance/protection property. The immediate counter is "nothing is Beatle-proof," but the working method is not a normal Beatle attack.
- `decanting`: Strong transformation/extraction clue. The trapped band emerges from a container like liquid poured out of a vessel.
- `colored telly` / `crystal`: Visual properties after the hole is applied: colored smoke/glass-like transformation, possibly relevant to color/channel/filter operations.
- `relativity`, `suspended`, `frozen`: John describes the figures as suspended/frozen in time/space, suggesting pause/freeze/frame/timestamp operations may be fair game.
